InfoSec 101

Here at The Verve Group we take information security seriously, being in the financial industry means that we can be an attractive target for cyber criminals looking to steal sensitive information such as financial details, client records, login credentials and more. 

By completing these steps you are helping to limit our overall attack surface making it much harder for cyber attacks to succeed and protecting the integrity of our data.

Strengthen your creds

It's important to use strong passwords for your accounts, whether these are online accounts or local.

Follow these steps to avoid being a victim of bruteforce attacks.

  • 12 characters long
  • Include a special character (!"£$%^&*()_+)
  • Include a capital letter
  • Include
  • at least 1 number
  • Use 2FA wherever possible
NEXT STEP

Lock your screen

This is good practice for infosec, simply locking your screen when leaving your PC unattended avoids people around you from viewing any sensitive information or taking complete control over your PC. 

This is good practice to follow in the office but more so when you are in a public place like Starbucks or on public transport.

Phishing attacks

Phishing is the number one strategy used by cyber criminals to infect PC's and networks, cyber criminals most commonly use email for there phishing attempts but this can also happen via phone and web. 

Phishing is an attempt in where a hacker tries to convince the end user into clicking a link or opening an attachment which includes malicous software, the email is usually composed of clever wording in an attempt to convince the user that the email is legitimate and will likely come from an address your familiar with ie your colleagues email address however there are still key signs we can pick up on to know it's malicous.

Hovering over the link shows a suspicous looking website
Spelling mistakes and no signature
